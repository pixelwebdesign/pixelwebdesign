You'll see a preview before anything goes live, and you'll have the chance to request changes. The whole thing is designed to be low-effort on your end and high-quality on mine.

Most projects are delivered within 7 to 14 days from the moment I receive your brief and deposit. Simpler landing pages can be ready in under a week. More complex multi-page sites may take up to two weeks. I'll give you a specific timeline in the proposal before anything starts.

Every project includes 2 weeks of support after launch. During that time I'll fix any bugs, make small adjustments, and make sure everything is running smoothly. After that, you can opt into ongoing support for NZD $40/month, which covers hosting, routine maintenance, and keeping the site running smoothly — or just reach out whenever you need something, and we'll arrange it separately at a fair rate.

The sites I build are custom-coded, so they don't come with a drag-and-drop editor. For most small businesses, this isn't an issue — updates are infrequent and I can handle them quickly at a fair rate. If you anticipate needing to update content regularly yourself (like a menu or blog), just mention it in your brief and I'll factor in the right solution from the start.

Every project is different, so pricing is assessed individually based on the size, complexity, and specific requirements of what you need. There's no fixed price list — instead, once I understand your project through the brief, I put together a clear proposal with a price that reflects the actual scope of work. We then discuss it together until we're both comfortable moving forward.

Once we've agreed on a price, I send over a simple contract that outlines the scope, timeline, and terms — so everything is clear from the start. Payment is split in two: 50% upfront to begin work, and the remaining 50% on delivery before the site goes live. The contract is provided after we've aligned on the project details and pricing.

You do — completely. Once the final payment is made, the code, the domain, and all the files are yours. I don't retain any rights to the work. Hosting and ongoing maintenance are handled separately — see the next two questions for how that works.

Once the final payment is made, you choose how things are set up. By default, your site stays live on my hosting infrastructure (GitHub and Vercel) under the ongoing hosting plan — the simplest option, since you don't need any technical know-how to keep things running. If you'd rather take full independent control, I can transfer the GitHub repository and hosting project directly into accounts you own, for a one-time $100 fee. Either way, you fully own the site, code, and design — this only affects who manages the hosting day-to-day. I'll walk you through both options before launch.

No — if you don't have one yet, I'll help you pick and register the right domain. If you already have one, we just point it to the new site at launch. Either way, it's something I take care of as part of the process.
